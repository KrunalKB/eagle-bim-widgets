What We Deliver

Core BIM Coordination Service Areas

Every BIM coordination services engagement covers the full scope — from model federation through final clearance. Here is what is included.

Federated Model Management

We aggregate architectural, structural, MEP, and civil models into a single federated environment using Navisworks or Revizto. Shared coordinates, agreed naming conventions, and disciplined file management ensure every discipline is working from the same reference — not separate silos.

Clash Detection and Resolution

Hard clash, soft clash, and clearance testing across all linked models. Every conflict is categorized by severity and discipline, ownership is assigned, resolution is documented, and the clash is re-tested after correction. Nothing is marked resolved without verification — full logs are maintained throughout the project.

MEP BIM Coordination Service

Our dedicated MEP BIM coordination service covers mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire protection, and HVAC systems. We coordinate duct routing, pipe runs, conduit, sprinkler layouts, and equipment clearances against all architectural and structural elements — producing clash-free MEP coordination drawings and coordinated models ready for fabrication.

Structural Opening and Penetration Coordination

Coordination of slab openings, beam penetrations, and MEP sleeves to ensure architectural and structural layouts align with MEP routing requirements. Penetration schedules, slab opening drawings, and sleeve placement plans produced directly from the coordinated model.

Coordination Meetings and Issue Management

We facilitate and attend virtual coordination meetings with all discipline teams — presenting clash reports, facilitating issue resolution, and capturing decisions. Every meeting produces updated coordination logs, re-test confirmations, and a clear list of outstanding items with owners and due dates.

Coordination Drawings and Reports

Our Process

How Our BIM Coordination Services Work

Every BIM coordination services project follows the same structured process — clear stages, clear ownership, no surprises.

01
Model Intake and Standards Setup
We receive all discipline models, verify shared coordinates and naming conventions, and establish the coordination environment in Navisworks or Revizto. Clash detection rules, clearance requirements, and reporting formats are agreed before the first test runs.
02
Clash Detection and Categorization
Hard and soft clash tests are run across all discipline pairs. Results are filtered, de-duplicated, and categorized by severity, discipline, and location. Duplicate and minor clashes are removed so the report your team receives contains only actionable issues.
03
Coordination Meeting and Resolution
Clash reports are presented to all discipline teams. Issues are reviewed, resolution responsibility is assigned, and deadlines are set. Our coordination team facilitates the discussion and captures every decision in the coordination log.
04
Model Update and Re-Testing
Disciplines update their models to resolve assigned clashes. We re-run clash detection to verify all resolutions and identify any new conflicts introduced by model changes. The cycle continues until all clashes are cleared.
05
Final Coordination Package
When all clashes are resolved, we deliver the final coordinated models, clash-free NWD file, coordination drawings, penetration schedules, and full resolution log — everything your construction team needs to build without surprises.
Types of Clashes We Detect
Type 01
Hard Clashes
Two building elements physically occupying the same space — a duct running through a structural beam, a pipe through a wall without a sleeve. These are critical and must be resolved before construction.
Type 02
Soft Clashes
Elements that do not physically intersect but violate required clearance zones — insufficient maintenance access to equipment, MEP services too close to structural elements, or inadequate ceiling zone depth.
Type 03
Workflow Clashes
Sequencing and constructability conflicts — elements that are geometrically clear but cannot be installed in the required construction sequence without removing previously installed components.

MEP Coordination Services

MEP Coordination Services Across All Building Systems

MEP coordination services are where the majority of site conflicts originate. Mechanical ductwork, electrical conduit, plumbing runs, and fire suppression systems all compete for the same ceiling zone — and when that coordination happens on paper rather than in a federated model, clashes reach the field as expensive surprises.

Our MEP BIM coordination service coordinates all building systems in 3D before a single component is installed. Duct routing is optimized for clearance and constructability, pipe runs are coordinated for slope and access, and every penetration is confirmed against structural framing. The result is a coordinated MEP model your fabricators and installers can trust from day one.

HVAC and Ductwork
Supply, return, exhaust duct routing, AHU connections, and diffuser layouts coordinated for clearance and constructability.
Mechanical Piping
Hydronic, chilled water, hot water, refrigerant, and condensate systems — slope, support, and clearance coordinated in 3D.
Plumbing Systems
Domestic water, waste, vent, and drainage coordination with slope requirements and penetration management.
Electrical and Conduit
Electrical conduit, cable tray, panel locations, and switchgear clearances coordinated against all other building systems.
Fire Protection
Sprinkler head layouts, main and branch pipe runs, and coverage zone verification coordinated with all ceiling elements.
Ceiling Zone and RCP
All MEP services fitted within the architectural ceiling zone — confirmed against the reflected ceiling plan for every area.
