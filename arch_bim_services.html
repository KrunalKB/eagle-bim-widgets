Interdisciplinary Coordination

Architectural Coordination with MEP and Structural Disciplines

Interdisciplinary coordination is where architectural projects are won or lost. Unresolved clashes between architectural, structural, and MEP models do not disappear — they surface as change orders, delays, and cost overruns on site. Eagle BIM manages the coordination process proactively as part of our architectural BIM services, starting at the model stage and not stopping until every issue is cleared.

We use Navisworks and Revizto for federated model reviews, resolving issues before they leave the model environment. Every coordination cycle produces a clear report with 3D issue snapshots that give all project stakeholders a precise, actionable picture of what needs to be resolved.

Start a Coordination Project →
Clash Detection and Resolution
Hard and soft clash testing across all linked models. Every clash is categorized, ownership is assigned, and issues are tracked through to final clearance with full resolution logs maintained throughout.
Structural Opening and Penetration Management
Coordination of structural beam penetrations, slab openings, and MEP sleeves to ensure architectural layouts align with structural framing and MEP routing requirements before documentation is finalized.
Ceiling Zone and MEP Clearance Coordination
Coordination of reflected ceiling plans with MEP services to confirm that ducts, pipes, and lighting fit within the architectural ceiling zone without conflict — keeping your RCP accurate and buildable.
Coordination Reports and Issue Logs
Structured coordination reports with 3D issue snapshots, resolution status, and full revision tracking. Delivered to all project stakeholders at every coordination milestone, so nothing falls through the cracks.

As-Built Documentation

As-Built and Record BIM Documentation

An accurate as-built record is the foundation for every renovation, expansion, and facility management decision that follows. Our architectural BIM services include incorporating field changes, redlines, and construction markups into final record models that reflect the building as it was actually constructed — not as it was designed.

As part of our architectural BIM services, we include site survey integration, point cloud registration, and as-built model verification. Deliverables support operations and maintenance manuals, facility management systems, and future capital projects, available in RVT, DWG, IFC, and PDF formats.
