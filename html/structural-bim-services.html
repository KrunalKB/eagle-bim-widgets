How We Work

Our Structural BIM Modeling Process

Every structural BIM services engagement follows the same structured process — from drawing intake through coordination-ready model delivery. Nothing is delivered without a model quality check against the source drawings.

01
Drawing Intake and Scope
Structural engineer's drawings, specifications, and BIM requirements received. LOD, coordinate system, Revit version, and model organisation agreed before any modeling begins.
02
Model Setup
Revit structural template configured, shared coordinates established, grids and levels set from the engineer's drawings, and structural families loaded to match section sizes specified.
03
Structural Modeling
All structural elements modeled — columns, beams, slabs, walls, foundations, openings, and connections — with correct section profiles, material properties, and analytical parameters throughout.
04
Model QA Check
Completed model checked against the structural drawings — element dimensions verified, section sizes confirmed, openings checked, levels validated, and coordination geometry reviewed before delivery.
05
Delivery and Handover
RVT, IFC, NWC, and DWG delivered with a model information sheet covering element counts, LOD achieved, and any noted discrepancies between drawings and model for EOR review.
